## 1. One-paragraph pitch

A rounded-voxel world built as two joined spirals. Below the surface, an idle excavation coils down through the strata of vanished ages while you are away, hauling up raw materials on a clock you do not control. Above, a second spiral of land unfurls outward as the dig deepens — a free-build sandbox where those materials, and only those materials, become your home, your town, your refuge. Scarcity comes from the dig. Meaning comes from the build. Crews live on the shaft walls; history is rebuilt where it fell. At the bottom of it all is the World Core, and the slow realization that the layers were left for you.

---

## 2. Design pillars (LOCKED)

1. **Scarcity makes placement sacred.** Every block on the surface was pulled from below. Nothing comes from a menu.
2. **The wait has a place to go.** Idle downtime is absorbed by building — and on dry days, by *drafting*: free ghost design that turns waiting into wanting. Never endured, never paid away.
3. **One tool, two verbs.** The Stitcher unmakes downward and remakes upward with the same gesture language.
4. **The past is the building material.** Narrative is carried by strata, artifacts, and rebuilt ruins, not cutscenes.

---

## 3. World geometry — the Mirrored Nautilus (CONCEPT-LOCKED)

The world is an island: one mountain, ringed by land, surrounded by ocean. Two spiral journeys mirror each other across the surface.

- **THE CARVE (above):** the player begins at the summit and carves the mountain downward. The route is a **partial nautilus** — roughly three-quarters of a turn per descent arc — that widens as it descends and ends at **THE BASE**. Every terrace cut is new habitable land; the carve IS the land unlock. Exposed cut faces show the age strata — the dig history is permanently visible on the skyline.
- **THE BORE (below):** an **ancient shaft** — found, not dug — drops from the summit through the mountain's heart and opens into **THE HOLLOW**: a vast cavern, its floor holding a still black lake, first seen by lantern light, possibly part-flooded and drainable. From its floor the player builds a **reverse-nautilus ramp climbing the inside**. Outside, you spiral down a mountain; inside, you spiral up its negative.
- **Ghost prefabs at monumental scale:** great halls, cisterns, and works off the Bore and within the Hollow, rebuilt in situ. Cisterns tie into the water system (5.7).
- **The coupling (LOCKED, restated):** land is earned only by the dig; the carve realizes the rule directly. The land is literally made of the age it exposes.
- **Freight asymmetry (CONCEPT-LOCKED):** lifts carry processed materials down automatically; timers are set in person. The daily commute up through your own carved history is the revisit engine — and under real-time surface light (6.6), the commute never looks the same twice.
- **New dig sites (LOCKED, carried):** depth thresholds open secondary digs as arms of the same persistent world. Prestige never resets the island.
- Open questions: wrap angle, terrace sizing, Hollow dimensions, climb exit, Base-level dig placement, age mapping between elevation and depth, water-table position within the geology.

## 4. The core loop (LOCKED)

**DESCEND → EXTRACT → ASCEND → REFINE → BUILD → WAIT → RETURN**

- **Descend:** workers have progressed while away; new strata may be revealed.
- **Extract:** the player makes the decisions machines can't — where to cut, what to shore, which vein to follow, blast vs. careful trace. Active play, 2–5 minutes. Careful extraction is the traced-seam gesture with haptic feedback (5.6) — the tactile signature of the game.
- **Ascend:** the haul arrives at the shaft head as **the Return Ritual** — carts up the hoist, crates opened, finds laid on the table, a crew note on the manifest. Ten seconds of theater; skippable once familiar.
- **Refine:** surface structures convert raw to refined on a second real-time clock.
- **Build:** free placement on the surface (and meaningfully below). On dry days, **draft**: free ghost blueprints anywhere, committed later with real material.
- **Wait / Return:** both clocks run offline; absence capped, never punished. Hard stops underground are the primary return hook; the crew note is its voice. Long lapses trigger the Lapse Ceremony (5.5) instead of a shrug.

Target sessions: 30 seconds (check the haul) to 20+ minutes (a build session). Both complete.

## 5. The dig (below)

### 5.1 Structure
- Two digs, one world: **the Carve** (mountain, outward, land-making) and **the Bore** (ancient, vertical, into the Hollow). The summit is the junction and permanent landmark.
- Strata are **authored content**: material identity, hazard identity, visual mood, narrative fragment. Elevation bands above; depth below.
- Depth is the progression number; it reads as story, not spreadsheet — and it reads *physically* in the Core Sample column at the shaft head.

### 5.2 Ages as strata (CONCEPT-LOCKED)
- Each stratum band is an **era** — its own architecture, signature materials, one unanswered question.
- The uncanny gradient is the narrative engine: nature, then craft, then intent.
- The five material families map onto the geology: Terrain, Timber, Masonry, Mineral, Metal — ore, then worked metal, then intent.
- Open: the age ladder itself. **Blocked on the HEM-canon decision (Section 0, OPEN).**

### 5.3 The vertical crew town (CONCEPT-LOCKED)
- Crews live on the shaft walls; the spiral's outer face is vertical real estate lit by the lantern string down the turns — the light layer (5.8) is what makes it read as home.
- Underground building is real building — same tools, materials, validation.
- Morale-as-dig-rate remains a Phase 0 hypothesis.
- **Crew timers (LOCKED):** player-assigned jobs, multiple durations, player-authored return schedule.
- Crew personality (notes, journals, possible named crew — see monetization) grows from the Return Ritual and Lapse Ceremony rather than from a simulation.

### 5.4 Ghost maps and rebuilding history (LOCKED)
- Chambers can reveal **ghost maps** — partial floor plans of what stood there, readable as blueprint ghost cells.
- **Rebuild in situ and it wakes:** age-specific refiners, cisterns (now mechanically alive via the water system), halls with permanent effects. Rewards are capability and story, never currency.
- Completed rebuilds contribute fragments to the Archive's story pages (9).
- Surface mirror: hauled signature materials seed era-styled rebuild missions above.

### 5.5 Idle mechanics
- Workers dig at a base rate, upgraded only by built structures.
- **Hard stops (LOCKED):** the dig pauses at decisions only the player can make — a void, a find, an unstable ceiling, a sealed door, **struck water**. "The dig is waiting on you" beats "numbers went up." The Return Ritual's crew note is how a hard stop speaks.
- Offline progress capped per absence (working: 8–12 productive hours).
- **The Lapse Ceremony (CONCEPT-LOCKED):** past a lapse threshold, the cap stops being the story. The crew's journal covers the absence; a survey report reveals a new find location or ghost map. Reactivation is where idle games quietly bleed out; here the lapse itself is a hook.
- **Support and collapse:** cut greedily and ceilings sag; shore with timber and masonry. **Collapse never destroys — it buries (LOCKED).** Re-excavate to recover everything intact; sometimes the fall opens what digging never would have. Water weakens supports (5.7), so the two hazards braid.

### 5.6 Careful extraction — the traced seam (CONCEPT-LOCKED)
- One gesture: trace the seam slowly with a finger. Haptics warn when the trace runs hot; the find comes free when it completes. No bars, no QTE.
- Blasting is one tap and gets bulk; the trace gets the find intact. The difference must live in the thumb.
- Phase 0 deliverable. iPad fallback (no haptics): visual seam-glow grammar.

### 5.7 Water (CONCEPT-LOCKED — the second hazard family)
- The dig can strike the **water table**: a hard stop. A careless breach floods the level — a detour, never a loss (flooding conceals; pumping recovers).
- Managed water is infrastructure: **pumps and channels** (Forge output with somewhere to go), wells, material-moving canals, mills that drive refiners.
- Water weakens supports: hazard interactions make the shoring game a system rather than a rule.
- The Hollow's black lake is the water system at cavern scale — part-flooded, drainable over the long game.
- Aquifer placement per age is authored, like all strata content.

### 5.8 Light (CONCEPT-LOCKED — the underground's expressive layer)
- Darkness is never hostile. Placed light is how underground space becomes *place*.
- Lanterns, sconces, and emissive premium materials are the deep game's expressive (and most desired) palette.
- The Hollow reveal is staged entirely by light. The crew town glows. The Bore is a thread of lamps in the dark.
- Whether any mechanic reads light level stays open; the layer earns its place on mood alone.

### 5.9 Rare finds (LOCKED)
- Rarity = depth plus care. Tiers: **Uncommon**, **Rare**, **Singular** (one of one, displayed not spent — displayed *in the Archive*, which is why the Archive cannot wait: without it, Singulars are dead inventory on day one).
- Premium materials in the 75-material taxonomy are the deep and singular tiers.

## 6. The surface (above)

### 6.1 Free build — and free drafting (LOCKED)
- Full sandbox with current tools. The constraint is inventory: you build with what you hauled and refined.
- **Drafting mode:** ghost blueprints placed freely at zero cost, anywhere. Committing spends real material, cell by cell or all at once. Drafts persist and total their material needs into a **want-list** that annotates the next dig with purpose. Scarcity converts from frustration into anticipation. Reuses ghost tech verbatim.

### 6.2 Land as reward (LOCKED)
- New surface land arrives only through depth. Never sold, never bought, never timered on its own.

### 6.3 Refining structures
- **Kiln, Sawmill, Forge, Cutter.** Throughput, queues, and layout matter. The Forge now also feeds the water chain: pumps, channels, mill parts (5.7).
- Rebuilt historic refiners extend the chain into age-specific processing.

### 6.4 Blueprint ghosts (LOCKED)
- Four faces of one mechanic: surface guidance, underground shoring requests, discovered ghost maps, and free drafting. Same tech, four meanings.

### 6.5 The shaft head
- The single mandatory structure; every world composes around it. Beside it stands **the Core Sample**: the glass column filling with the true cross-section of every stratum passed — the game's signature object, the vertical world's map, and the answer players give to "how deep are you." The Phase 0 Archive shelf lives here until the hall is built.

### 6.6 Day and night (CONCEPT-LOCKED)
- The surface runs on the real clock: lamplit evenings, gold mornings. The underground is timeless.
- Ascending feels like *surfacing*; every return looks different; the freight commute becomes a scene instead of a route.

## 7. Material economy (LOCKED principles)

- **Circulation, not currency.** Supply flows up; timber and masonry flow back down as shoring; water flows where you channel it.
- **Raw vs. refined** doubles the roster without new art families.
- **Nothing rare is purchasable. No coins. Ever.**
- Scarcity tuning target: a daily 10-minute player banks 60–120 placed blocks/day early. A room, not a castle. Castles take weeks and therefore mean something. Drafting absorbs the gap between appetite and inventory.
- Overflow valve: capped commons auto-convert to bulk needs.

---

## 8. Progression

- **Depth milestones** are chapter breaks: reveal, material, narrative, surface unfurl — framed as season finales.
- **Prestige = a new shaft (LOCKED):** a new spiral arm of the same world. Veteran bonuses carry; the town persists.
- Long-run: multiple shafts converge on the same deep target. The World Core is not under one shaft; it is under all of them.

## 9. Narrative layer

- Light-touch Correspondence structure: the strata are a message; depth is reading order; the ages are its chapters.
- **The Archive is where the reading accumulates:** strata fragments assemble into story pages; Singulars stand on pedestals; empty pedestals ask their quiet question. Environmental delivery everywhere else — artifacts, impossible strata, ghost maps that are not the player's engineering, the sealed door built from the other side.
- The World Core stays glimpsed, not explained, through v1.
- Tone: warm, strange, patient. Kintsugi carried from Hem — the town is the mended thing, seams showing, made of every age below it.

## 10. Monetization (v3 — LOCKED principles, revised stack)

**The line (LOCKED):** money buys more game and more looks. It never buys rarity, land, or time-skips on finds.

**The stack:**
1. **Subscription — primary model (LOCKED).** Breadth and convenience: additional crew, more refine queues, expanded storage, a second concurrent shaft, stats/archive niceties.
2. **Named crew as one-time character purchases (PROMOTED).** Individual crew members sold directly — each with a name, voice, banter, and a job specialty. No gacha, no duplicates, no trunks: the buy-the-character model, once, owned forever. This is breadth with personality; it partially answers the worker-fiction question with revenue attached; their voices plug straight into the Return Ritual notes and Lapse journals. The strongest clean lever in the research.
3. **Seasonal expedition shafts.** Paid content drops: a new age, new strata, new story. Cadence open.
4. **Event-limited cosmetic finishes (PROMOTED).** Patinas, glazes, dressings inside seasonal expeditions — same materials, different skin. Time-limited expression, never time-limited capability.
5. **Refine-queue time skips (PROMOTED, tightly scoped).** Skips apply to refining queues ONLY — never the dig, never finds, never land. The two lowest-corrosion paid items in the historical record are 4 and 5, and both are now in.
6. **Rewarded ads, opt-in only,** for non-subscribers. Removed by subscription.
7. **One-time Patron/Founder purchase:** removes ads forever plus a cosmetic family.
8. Extra world slots.

**Never:** coins, loot boxes, material packs, energy systems, purchasable rares or land, gacha of any kind, gating automation behind monetized collection, protective purchases, piggy-bank mechanics — **and pinch-point offers, named here permanently:** no purchase surface may ever appear at a hard stop, a collapse, or a flood. The reason is product, not just ethics: the entire pitch is that scarcity is honest. The moment a player sees a buy-out offer at a collapse, they retroactively understand every collapse as a sales event, and pillar 1 dies in their head even if the math never changed. Dark patterns are survivable in a game about greasy money; they are fatal in a game whose soul is that the blocks were earned.

**Onboarding (LOCKED):** no store surface in the first sessions. Attachment first, ask later.

**Business goal (working):** durable five-figure months by month 18.

## 12. Scope phases

One crude crew-morale test. Success test: does DOWN, UP, WAIT, RETURN breathe — *starting from minute one*? Nothing widens until yes.

### Phase 1 — Vertical slice
Fifteen strata across two ages. All four refiners. Support/collapse with the burial rule. **Water table + first flood/pump chain.** Rare finds with careful extraction. First ghost map + in-situ rebuild. First surface unfurl. ### Phase 2 — Retention proof
30 days of content. Telemetry on day-7/day-30, the unfurl moment, **and lapse-reactivation rates specifically.** Subscription live. Named-crew purchases live if the crew fiction has proven warm. The only question: does retention justify building more?

### Phase 3 — The long game
Second shaft. Deep ages. World Core glimpses. Seasonal expedition pipeline. Crew fiction deepens.

### Explicitly NOT in scope soon
Multiplayer, marketplace, procedural strata, combat, hunger/survival, coins, full citizen simulation, Android before iOS proves out.

## 13. Risks, plainly

1. **Idle math is unforgiving.** Copy proven curve structures; innovate on meaning, not math.
2. **Authored ages are a content treadmill.** Front-load 30 days; drafting and the crew town carry the tail.
3. **Now five systems** (idle, sandbox, production, crew/morale, water). Mitigation: one renderer, one tool, one gesture language; water reuses the validation pipeline's spatial logic; morale stays a hypothesis until Phase 0 earns it.
4. **Cannibalizes HEM attention.** Standalone product, own P&L, shared engine, separate roadmap.
5. **Spiral geometry could fight the builder.** Test early whether the spiral reads as gift or cage.
6. **Scarcity frustration.** Overflow valve, generous commons, drafting as the pressure release, honest store framing.
7. **Ritual fatigue.** The Return Ritual and Lapse Ceremony must stay ten-second scenes with instant skips, or the game's warmth becomes its friction. Theater is seasoning, not a course.

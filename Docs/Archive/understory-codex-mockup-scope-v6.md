# UNDERSTORY
## Codex Mockup Scope v6
### Outside-current-work exploratory scope
### Date: 3 Jul 2026

Purpose: build fast mockups that test the feeling of Understory before committing to production systems. This is a creative/product scope for Codex Chief to translate into build tasks. It does not require changing the technical stack, branch strategy, or current implementation plan.

---

## 1. Mockup goal

Prove that Understory works as an iOS-first tapper-builder with epic implication and intimate touch.

The mockups should answer:

1. Does the first 10-minute loop breathe?
2. Does rounded voxel placement still feel premium and tactile?
3. Does free drafting turn scarcity into wanting instead of frustration?
4. Does the Return Ritual feel warm and authored, not like a claim screen?
5. Does clearing the Veil make land unlock feel earned?
6. Can the story be felt through props, layers, and rituals without lore dumping?

---

## 2. Core experience to mock

### First 10-minute flow

1. **Summit Refuge above the Veil**
   - Player sees a cramped high-altitude settlement.
   - The lower world is hidden by a cloud/haze ocean.
   - Far-Glass exists but cannot yet see clearly.
   - Sealed Bore sits at the center, feared and important.

2. **Bore opens**
   - The sealed cap shifts, cracks, or is uncovered.
   - Player sees darkness, old stone, and a vertical drop.
   - No full exposition.

3. **First descent**
   - Small authored dig chamber.
   - One common material seam.
   - One ancient hint: carved duct, old lungstone, star mark, or impossible smooth joint.

4. **Trace seam**
   - Player traces a glowing seam slowly.
   - Fast movement creates visual flare and warning feedback.
   - Successful trace yields intact material or first small Singular.

5. **Return Ritual**
   - Hoist/carts/crates arrive at shafthead.
   - Haul table displays material physically.
   - Crew note gives warmth: short, human, and specific.

6. **Refine**
   - Kiln or first refiner converts raw clay/stone into buildable blocks.
   - No complicated recipe UI.

7. **Build / commit draft**
   - Player sees a ghost draft for a small shafthead wall, shelter, stair, or lantern post.
   - They commit blocks using real refined material.
   - Placement feels soft, polished, and satisfying.

8. **Core Sample and Archive seed**
   - Core Sample gains first band.
   - Archive shelf receives one fragment or Singular.

9. **Want-list appears**
   - A bigger draft remains unfinished.
   - Game makes the next dig meaningful: “needs 24 dressed stone” or equivalent.

## 4. Visual style targets

### General

- Rounded voxel blocks, polished and tactile.
- Premium but not toy-like.
- Warm under pressure.
- Handmade human surfaces over ancient machine-temple geometry.
- Kintsugi logic: damage remains visible, reinforced, and beautiful.

### Summit palette

- Wind-worn ceramic.
- Pale stone.
- Woven insulation.
- Salvaged metal.
- Warm lantern amber.
- Cold Veil light below.

### Ancient Breathwright palette

- Ritual stone.
- Black carbon vault material.
- Mineral thread.
- Soft inner glow.
- Pressure gates.
- Water bowls.
- Star shafts.

### Hollower overlay

- Patchwork.
- Bedrolls.
- Warning paint.
- Emergency braces.
- Proto-summit language marks.
- Small domestic traces in giant ancient rooms.

### Current crew overlay

- Fresh chalk.
- New timber braces.
- Lantern strings.
- Haul tags.
- Simple tools.
- Warm human notes.

## 6. Economy mockup

The economy prototype can be simple. It needs to show pressure and relief, not final balance.

### Needed states

- Raw material inventory.
- Refined material inventory.
- Draft material need.
- One refiner queue.
- One crew job timer.
- One hard stop.
- One rare find.

### Starting values for feel testing

- First authored haul gives enough for 12 to 24 committed blocks.
- First draft asks for 40 to 60 blocks total.
- Player should feel accomplishment and a visible shortage.
- Short job: 10 to 15 minutes.
- Work shift: 1 to 2 hours.
- Overnight job: 8 hours.
- Early daily target: 60 to 120 placed block equivalents.

### Questions to answer

- Does the player understand why they are short on material?
- Does the want-list make the next dig feel self-authored?
- Does the refiner feel like useful pacing or a blocker?
- Does the Return Ritual make the haul feel physical?

---

## 7. Monetization mockup boundaries

Do not surface monetization in the first mockup flow.

Later monetization UX mockups should test calm purchase surfaces only:

- Archive cosmetic shelf.
- Crew lodge / named crew roster.
- Season expedition board.
- Settings / support founder bundle.

Never mock a purchase prompt at:

- hard stop
- collapse
- flood
- shortage moment
- rare find
- land unlock
- story gate

---

## 8. Acceptance criteria

The mockups pass if a tester can describe the game without a lore explanation:

- “We live above a dangerous fog.”
- “The shaft gives us materials.”
- “The old machines clear land.”
- “I build with what I bring up.”
- “The mountain has older history inside it.”
- “I want to come back because my draft needs material.”

The mockups fail if the user says:

- “This is Minecraft underground.”
- “This is an idle claim screen.”
- “This is a city builder with lore pasted on.”
- “I do not know why the fog clears.”
- “The story seems too big for the mechanics.”
- “I feel blocked because I am waiting.”

## 10. Suggested work packets for Codex Chief

### Packet 1: First 10-minute clickable flow

Deliverable: a clickable prototype or lightweight in-engine flow covering Summit -> Bore -> first seam -> Return Ritual -> refine -> commit draft -> Core Sample.

Acceptance: a viewer can feel the game loop without reading this doc.

### Packet 2: Touch interaction sandbox

Deliverable: a small test pad with rounded voxel placement, ghost drafting, commit, remove/lift, material tray, and trace seam.

Acceptance: placement and trace feel good before any economy exists.

### Packet 3: Veil and Old Lung beat

Deliverable: before/after scene showing broken Breathline, player completion, Old Lung pulse, Veil thinning, terrace reveal.

Acceptance: dig-unlocks-land feels physical and emotional.

### Packet 4: Economy paper prototype

Deliverable: spreadsheet or simple data table for D1, D3, D7, D30 targets.

Acceptance: shows how 60 to 120 early daily placed block equivalents can happen without paid relief.

### Packet 5: Style tile pass

Deliverable: 4 visual tiles: Summit Present, Breathwright Origin, Hollower Overlay, Wire/Digital Edge.

Acceptance: each layer is visually readable at iPad scale.

### Packet 6: Archive/Core Sample object pass

Deliverable: static or interactive objects for Core Sample and Archive shelf.

Acceptance: progress feels physical, collectible, and screenshot-worthy.

Build confidence, not breadth.

The first mockups should make the player feel: I touched the mountain, brought something old back into the light, and used it to make home a little more possible.
